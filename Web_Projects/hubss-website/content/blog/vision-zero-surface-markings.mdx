# Vision Zero and the Role of High-Visibility Surface Markings

Vision Zero — the commitment to eliminating traffic fatalities and serious injuries — has moved from Scandinavian traffic policy into mainstream Canadian municipal planning. Cities including Toronto, Vancouver, Edmonton, and Ottawa have adopted formal Vision Zero frameworks. As these programs roll out, high-visibility pavement markings are proving to be among the most cost-effective interventions available.

## The Evidence Base

Research from Transport Canada and the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety consistently demonstrates that high-visibility crosswalk markings — particularly continental (ladder) style markings in bright thermoplastic — reduce pedestrian-involved collisions by 30–50% at uncontrolled intersections.

The mechanism is straightforward: high-contrast markings increase driver awareness of pedestrian crossing zones, reduce vehicle speeds as drivers approach marked crossings, and increase pedestrian confidence — encouraging use of designated crossings rather than mid-block jaywalking.

## Beyond Standard Markings

Traditional painted crosswalk lines fade within 12–18 months in Canadian conditions, requiring regular repainting. Each repainting cycle comes with traffic management costs, material costs, and service interruption. High-visibility thermoplastic markings — such as HUB's TrafficPatterns and TrafficPatternsXD systems — maintain retroreflectivity for 5–7 years, dramatically reducing lifecycle costs while delivering consistent safety performance.

For the highest-risk intersections, aggregate-reinforced thermoplastic provides both visual prominence and tactile feedback — creating a physical texture change that alerts distracted pedestrians and drivers alike.

## The AODA Connection

Ontario's Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA) and similar provincial frameworks require that pedestrian infrastructure be accessible to people with visual and mobility impairments. High-contrast surface markings, combined with tactile warning surfaces, create the multi-sensory environment that AODA compliance demands.

HUB Surface Systems works with municipal accessibility coordinators to design crosswalk and pedestrian zone treatments that meet or exceed AODA requirements while delivering the visual impact that makes Vision Zero interventions effective.

## ROI for Municipalities

The economic case for investing in high-quality surface markings is compelling. A single pedestrian fatality costs a municipality an estimated $11–14 million in direct and indirect costs. A high-visibility thermoplastic crosswalk installation at a dangerous intersection costs $3,000–$8,000 — and lasts 5–7 years.

The math is unambiguous. For municipalities serious about Vision Zero, upgraded surface markings should be among the first items in any safety capital budget.
