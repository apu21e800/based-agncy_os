# Why Stamped Asphalt Outperforms Concrete in Canadian Winters

Decorative concrete gets all the attention in landscape architecture magazines and municipal planning documents. But when the temperature drops below -20°C and the freeze-thaw cycle begins, stamped asphalt consistently outperforms its rigid counterpart. After 30 years of delivering decorative surface solutions across Canada, HUB Surface Systems has seen this play out in municipality after municipality.

## The Freeze-Thaw Problem

Concrete is inherently brittle. When water penetrates micro-cracks and freezes, it expands with enormous force — enough to fracture concrete from the inside out. Canadian municipalities spend billions annually repairing frost-damaged concrete infrastructure. Decorative concrete is even more vulnerable: the surface coatings and sealers that create the aesthetic finish are often the first casualties of freeze-thaw cycling.

Asphalt, by contrast, is flexible. It moves with temperature changes rather than fighting them. A well-designed stamped asphalt surface — like StreetPrint — can flex through hundreds of freeze-thaw cycles without cracking or spalling.

## Maintenance Cost Comparison

A 2019 study by the Municipal Engineers Association found that decorative concrete pedestrian infrastructure required remediation work within an average of 7 years in Canadian climate zones. Stamped asphalt installations using modern thermoplastic overlays routinely achieve 15–20 years of service life with only routine seal coat maintenance.

The numbers are stark: over a 20-year lifecycle, municipalities typically spend 40–60% less on stamped asphalt maintenance compared to equivalent decorative concrete installations.

## Repairability

When concrete fails, it fails completely — requiring full removal and replacement. Asphalt failures are localized and patchable. A damaged section of stamped asphalt can be milled out and replaced without visible seams or colour mismatches, especially with modern coloured overlay systems.

## HUB's Approach

HUB Surface Systems' StreetPrint system was specifically developed for Canadian climate conditions. The in-place stamping process works with existing asphalt infrastructure, eliminating the need for removal and replacement. The StreetBond colour coating applied over the stamped surface uses UV-stable pigments that maintain colour fidelity through 20+ Canadian winters.

For municipalities considering decorative surface treatments, the choice is clear: stamped asphalt delivers the aesthetic impact of decorative concrete at lower installation cost, lower lifecycle cost, and with far greater resilience to the Canadian climate.
